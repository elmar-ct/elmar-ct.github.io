Chap3: Money, or the circulation of Commodities

1) The measure of value

Money as a measure of value is the necessary form of appearance of the measure of value which is immanent in commodities, namely labour-time.

As measure of value and as standard of price, money performs two quite different functions. It is the measure of value as the social incarnation of human labour; it is the standard of price as a quantity of metal with a fixed weight.

The prices, or quantities of gold, into wich the values of commodities are ideally changed are therefore now expressed in the money-name, or the legally valid names or the subdivisions of the gold standard made for the purpose of recknoning.

Price is the money-name of the labour objectified in a commodities.
