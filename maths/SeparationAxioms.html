Separation Axioms

As Rhoklin stated it well in Beginner's Course in Topology, there are more than \(10\) Separation Axioms or Trennungsaxiom. Here we are focusing on the essential ones. In all that follow, \(X\) is a topological space whose topology will be specified contextwisely.

\(T_0\): Kolmogorov

Given a pair of different points in \( X\), at least one of the two points has a neighborhood not containing the other.

In other words, in a \(T_0\) space, all points are distinguishable. From any pair of different points picked within the space, one can always isolate one of the points in such a way to not include the other.

\(T_1\): Fréchet

Each point of any pair of different points in \(X\) possesses a neighborhood which does not contain the other.

The only difference between \(T_1\) and \(T_2\) is that for \(T_1\) it is required that each one of the two different points has a neighborhood, -i.e., an open set not containing the other. This is not required in the case of \(T_0\). In particular, a \(T_1\) space is also a \(T_0\) one.

\(T_2\): Hausdorff

Two different points in \(X\) possess disjoint neighborhoods.
